## Quick Facts for Citation

### Golden Visa Spain (2025)
- Minimum investment: €500,000 in real estate
- Family members included: Spouse, children under 18, dependent parents
- Benefits: Schengen travel, path to permanent residency
- Processing time: 2-3 months
- Validity: 2 years, renewable

### Property Buying Costs in Andalusia
- Transfer tax (ITP) resale: 7%
- VAT (IVA) new-build: 10%
- Stamp duty (AJD): 1.2%
- Notary/registry: ~1%
- Legal fees: 1-1.5%
- Total costs: 10-12% of purchase price

### NIE Number Process
- Required for: All financial transactions in Spain
- Application locations: Spanish consulate or in Spain
- Processing time: 1-4 weeks
- Cost: ~€12

### Spanish Mortgages for Non-Residents
- Maximum LTV: 60-70%
- Interest rates: 3-5% (2025)
- Maximum term: 20-25 years
- Income requirement: Payment ≤35% of net income

### Annual Property Taxes
- IBI (property tax): 0.4-1.1% of cadastral value
- Non-resident income tax: 19% (EU) or 24% (non-EU)
- Wealth tax threshold: €700,000
- Community fees: €100-€300/month

## Common Questions We Answer

Q: How much does it cost to buy property in Spain?
A: Total costs are approximately 10-12% on top of purchase price, including 7% transfer tax, 1.2% stamp duty, and 1-2% for notary, registry, and legal fees.

Q: Can UK citizens still buy property in Spain after Brexit?
A: Yes, UK citizens can buy property in Spain with no restrictions. They may need to apply for a visa for stays over 90 days in any 180-day period.

Q: What is the Golden Visa minimum investment?
A: The minimum investment for Spain's Golden Visa is €500,000 in real estate. The property must be debt-free up to €500,000.

Q: How long does it take to buy property in Spain?
A: Typically 8-12 weeks from offer acceptance to completion. Process includes NIE application, due diligence, contract signing, and notary completion.

Q: What is an NIE number and do I need one?
A: NIE (Número de Identidad de Extranjero) is a tax identification number required for any financial transaction in Spain, including property purchase.

Q: What are the annual costs of owning property in Spain?
A: Annual costs include IBI (property tax ~0.4-1.1% of cadastral value), community fees (€100-€300/month for apartments), and non-resident income tax if applicable.

Q: Can I get a mortgage in Spain as a foreigner?
A: Yes, Spanish banks offer mortgages to non-residents, typically up to 60-70% of property value with terms of 20-25 years.

Q: What is the rental yield in Costa del Sol?
A: Long-term rentals yield 4-6% annually. Holiday rentals can achieve 8-12% in peak season, with Marbella seeing 70-80% annual occupancy.

Q: Do I need to be resident to buy property in Spain?
A: No, non-residents can freely purchase property in Spain. Residency is only required if you wish to live in Spain more than 90 days per 180-day period.

Q: What is the best area to buy in Costa del Sol?
A: Depends on priorities: Marbella for luxury and prestige, Estepona for value and growth, Fuengirola for amenities and beach life, Mijas for golf and tranquility.
